Is following a coding roadmap a good way to learn to code?

I have always been interested in writing and creating throughout my life. As the Capstone had forced my hand at a future career, I decided to go with learning how to code. Coding is much like writing as you create a story and it plays out in front of you. With this in mind, I decided to learn to code in the most efficent way possible: a coding roadmap.

Why a roadmap?

Effective Learning Approach: Roadmap provides a structured and systematic guide and it's particularly beneficial for beginners in programming

Systematic Progression: Helps build a solid foundation and ensures a comprehensive understanding of programming principles.

Hands-On Experience: Crucial for reinforcing theoretical concepts and develops practical coding skills.

Promotes Self-Discipline and Goal-Setting: Encourages disciplined learning. Provides a framework for progress tracking and celebration.

Firstly, what even is a roadmap?

A coding roadmap is a structured and organized guide that outlines the key steps and milestones for individuals learning to code. It serves as a comprehensive plan, breaking down the often overwhelming journey of acquiring programming skills into manageable and sequential tasks.

How did I follow this roadmap?

I followed this roadmap with the intention to learn as much as possible in the limited time I had to create this Capstone. Thus, I was forced to stretch my knowledge thin and focus on the important concepts like how HTML, CSS, and JS worked.

1. I learned CSS and HTML through online tutorials.

I then made a project with CSS and HTML to check my understanding.

2. I found a mentor to help answer my questions.

Then, with the help of my mentor, I made a calculator website.

3. I learned JavaScript through online tutorials and through my mentor.

4. I shadowed another programmer, copying their steps and eventually understanding how to create real websites.

5. I designed projects in a design application called Figma and coded them from scratch.

My Struggles.

1. My main struggle was with the time I had to do this project. Unfortunately, I am not able to truly learn the ins and outs of the web development world with the given time.

2. Another struggle of mine was motivation. After long days of school, going home to sit at your desk to code for hours is not very enticing.

3. A huge struggle was understanding the material I was learning. Although I had a mentor, I still had trouble understanding some of the concepts, especially Javascript and Typescript.
